# 🛡️ Part A – Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

## 📌 Overview
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a rapidly growing cybercrime in India where attackers trick users into authorizing fraudulent transactions. With the popularity of apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, and Paytm, such scams have become increasingly common among college students and young professionals.

## ⚙️ How the Crime Happens (Step-by-Step)
1. The scammer contacts the victim via call, SMS, or social media pretending to be a buyer, bank official, or service provider.  
2. They build trust and create urgency (e.g., “You will lose your account” or “I need to pay you now”).  
3. The victim is sent a “collect request” or a fake payment link.  
4. The scammer instructs the victim to enter their UPI PIN, claiming it is required to receive money.  
5. Once the victim enters the PIN, money is debited from their account instead of being credited.  

## 🎯 Target Audience
- College students unfamiliar with financial security  
- First-time digital payment users  
- People who frequently use online marketplaces  

## ⚠️ Consequences
- Immediate financial loss  
- Unauthorized access to bank accounts  
- Emotional stress and loss of trust in digital platforms  
- Difficulty in recovering stolen funds  

## 🧾 Example Scenario (Fictional)
Rohit, a college student, listed his old phone online. A scammer posing as a buyer sent him a payment request and guided him to “accept” it by entering his UPI PIN. Within seconds, ₹10,000 was deducted from his account.
